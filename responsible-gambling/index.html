Responsible Gaming

Responsible gaming is about informed choices, clear limits, and recognising when play is no longer enjoyable. We encourage setting time and budget boundaries before starting and reviewing them regularly. Awareness of risk factors and honest self-assessment help maintain balance with other priorities. Where features simulate chance-based experiences, they should be approached for entertainment rather than financial expectations. If play stops being fun or creates stress, we want you to know the tools and support available. These principles apply to experiences that reference chickenroad.

Warning signs may include hiding play from friends, borrowing to fund sessions, or neglecting work and relationships. Feeling anxious when not playing, chasing losses, or breaking self-set rules are additional indicators. Rapidly increasing stakes or playing longer than planned can suggest loss of control. Recommendations for responsible gambling

Set a clear spending limit you can comfortably afford and do not treat play as a source of income. Decide in advance how long a session will last and use timers to take regular breaks. Avoid playing when tired, upset, or under the influence, as judgement may be impaired. Keep entertainment separate from essential finances and never borrow to continue a session. If limits are repeatedly exceeded, step back and reassess your plan before returning. These suggestions accompany sections that reference chicken road.
